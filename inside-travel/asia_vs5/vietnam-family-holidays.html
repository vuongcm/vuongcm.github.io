The Best Vietnam beaches for family to relax

Beautiful beach is not to be missed in any family holiday plan, and especially when in Vietnam. With long sandy beaches, filled with spectacular coconut, relaxing at the beach with a delicious, freshly prepared refreshing cocktail and watching your kids playing with the winds and waves, you will certainly find paradise here. Let’s take a look at some of the best beaches for family in Vietnam

Phu Quoc Beaches

For families who love the tranquility and peaceful moments, Phu Quoc will be mentioned as one of the most suitable places for your vacation.

Located in the Gulf of Thailand, Phu Quoc is famous for its extraordinary beauty with unspoiled white-sand beaches and quaint fishing villages. Customers can easily take direct flight from Ho Chi Minh City or Hanoi Capital to get there.

Visiting to this beautiful islands, your family will have chance to not only enjoy the charming sunny beaches, relax in the glorious resorts with the scenic views over the islands, but taste delicious seafood with affordable price. More interestingly, your families can choose to discover the charming island in different forms: from cycling tours along the winding roads, go scuba diving around the coral reefs, take a trip to the historic Phu Quoc prison and learn about the history, or experience the daily life of fishermen with a stop at a traditional fish sauce factory to see how to made the Phu Quoc fish sauce.

Nha Trang Beaches

Known as a metropolitan coastal beautiful city and a center of politic, economy, culture, technology, science and tourism of Khanh Hoa Province with a favorable climate by nature with a fast social development, Nha Trang is listed among the most famous seaside towns of Vietnam for beach lover year round.

Your family will be offered the most relaxing holiday with lovely white-sand beaches and interesting scuba diving activities. Ocean ecosystem will surprise your kids by beautiful views of coral reefs, flooded forests, sea-grasses inhabited with a diverse of marine creatures. Otherwise, many islands in remote area such as Mun Island, Nhieu Island or Tam Island will surprise you by the charming unspoiled natural beauty ecosystem, a tropical green forest surrounding romantic stretching white sand.

Top 10 Vietnamese foods to try during Vietnam family holiday

"If you reject the food, ignore the customs, fear the religion and avoid the people, you might better stay at home." - James A. Michener

When travelling to Vietnam, many travelers fall in love with the food, not only from the fine-dining restaurant, but also in the local street vendor. Try to taste some Vietnam local cuisine and the tasty flavor may make you may really miss us when coming back to your home country

Pho

How would your holiday to Vietnam be completed without Pho ? Easily to be found in many streets in Vietnam, Pho consists of rice noodles, a light of beef of chicken, some green herbs. Simple but tasty, and available at all meals

Bun Cha

A Hanoi specialty that appeals to even the US President Obama in his recent visit to Vietnam in 2016: Bun Cha - with fatty pork grilled on a charcoal brazier and noodle served with fresh herb, bean sprouts and a slightly sweetish sauce.

Banh Xeo

Banh Xeo is one of the most favorite dishes of tourists when coming to Vietnam. It is made with rice flour, coconut milk, egg, and then fried with pork, shrimp, and a bunch of bean sprouts, and to complete the dish, you will wrap up the cake with lettuce or the rice paper.

Even don’t miss the chance to make a do-it-yourself banh xeo when joining the tour to rural area in Hoi An Ancient Town, you will be surprised by your cooking talent and the delicious flavor

Banh Mi

Your trip to Vietnam can be much localized when tasting Banh Mi – one of the most popular dishes of all classes in Vietnam. The children eat Banh Mi as the breakfast before going to school, the office staff can choose Banh Mi for their lunch, the young boys have Banh Mi after his soccer match in the afternoon, and the night-shift worker can bring Banh Mi to have energy for working.

You can choose your own favorite fillings like pork belly, fish cakes, meatballs, egg, creamy pate and veggies: carrots, cilantro, cucumber, chili sauces. Maybe a pretty magical dish is waiting for you to explore.

Fresh Spring Roll (Nem Cuon / Goi Cuon)

A refreshing change from the usual fried dishes, with a simple recipe, tourist can add grated carrots and bean sprouts, shrimp, pork, and herbs then wrap over and rolling the fillings and don’t forget to dip into the sweet sauce. It is said that you are tasting a relaxing summer in your mouth, isn’t it ?

Fried Spring Roll (Nem Ran)

Vietnamese spring rolls are not made as daily dishes in every Vietnam families’ meals but are prepared on important occasions like Tet Holiday, or exclusive family reunion time, or when welcoming special guests. Besides carrot, bean sprout, dried noodle and egg, the minced pork as main ingredients can be replaced with either minced beef or minced seafood, depending on your choice. Don’t forget the fish sauce used as a dip for spring roll and you will be surprised by the harmony of ingredient in your mouth.

Cao Lau

When coming to Hoi An, don’t miss the chance to try Cao Lau - a special traditional dish that cannot be found anywhere outside the town. More than a mixture of the yellow steamed noodle, pork, and local herb, Cao Lau holds great mystery about the origin: only secret family recipe, with the water from Ba Le well and the local special ash from Cham Island, the dish deserve the attention of your family when enjoying Vietnam Holiday

Cha Ca

Enjoy the unique experience of making a wonderful dish right in your table. Your children will be surely excited about creating the dishes themselves by rolling many things together: vegetables, rice noodles, with peanuts, onion, shrimp sauce and especially delicious fried sliced fishes which have been processed complicatedly to maintain the fresh with numerous ingredients for the excellent taste.

Mi Quang

It doesn’t matter what ingredients to chose: pork, shrimp, chicken, snakehead fish, jellyfish or a vegetarian option, filled with a secret mix sauce, rice crackers, lemon, peanuts and fresh local herbs, Mi Quang is one of the greatest local noodles that is worth tasting in your Vietnam Family Holiday.

Hue Beef Vermicelli Noodle (Bun Bo Hue)

A popular Vietnamese dish containing rice noodle and beef which is greatly favored for spicy, sour, salty and sweet balance that your family can choose for every meal.

Planning Tips for your Vietnam family holidays

Best time and weather in Vietnam

When considering time to go to Vietnam, it poses a difficult question to get the best answer as there is not really a right or wrong time to visit Vietnam without experiencing not only the sunny days but some rain in different quantities.

Known as a tropical climate country, Vietnam is typically humid and hot, the temperature and weather can change considerably along the country. While there is monsoon in the North and people need wearing layers of sweaters and jacket in the mountainous North, many visitors are still enjoying the Southern sunny beaches.

As the locals, our travel consultants are very happy to share you the recommendation for a most suitable schedule. Make sure that you have told our staff your wishes and we will try our best to assist you better.

Physical Demands for children

The itinerary and the time frame is designed based on the balance of activities and relaxation then it is not too hard for kids to join and play during the tour at the most comfort. As the schedule is outlined based on the general knowledge and experience while working with kids, it would be much better if parents can take consideration into the itinerary and share us any of your idea for the most suitable itinerary for our family.

Vietnam Visa for family members (Air, Cruise)

A Passport with more than six months validity and Vietnam Visa is required for most foreigners who are willing to come to Vietnam. Please check with the Vietnamese embassy in your country to get Vietnam Visa prior to your departure.

In case you would like to get a Vietnam Visa on Arrival at the airport, we can support our family to arrange the Vietnam Visa Approval Letter. When receiving it, you will print and show it when arriving at the airport, paying the stamp fee and get Vietnam Visa. Vietnam Visa On Arrival is only accepted for the air entry.

Please kindly contact with our travel consultant for a better advice regarding the Vietnam Visa Service.

Suitable clothes when travelling to Vietnam

You will join various activities in the trip to Vietnam, so light clothes maybe recommended. Moreover, the weather can change and the temperature can vary among the places. Don’t forget to get the tips to prepare clothes from our travel consultants

Money in Vietnam, Food in Vietnam

The currency in Vietnam is Vietnam Dong. You can easily exchange the money in the booth at the airport, or in some stores in the Old Quarter. Your guide will help you with that, no worries.

The food in Vietnam is now become more and more popular in the world and many travelers fall in love with the local dishes when visiting Vietnam. With Vietnam Family Holiday packages, we try to introduce the local delicious dishes to families with qualified quality. If there is any food requirement or special diet, we will try our best to fulfill the request, so make sure to advise us when making the booking
